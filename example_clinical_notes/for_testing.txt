Example 1: Gastroenterology
PROCEDURE: Esophagogastroduodenoscopy (EGD).,PREOPERATIVE DIAGNOSIS: Dysphagia.,POSTOPERATIVE DIAGNOSIS: Severe erosive esophagitis, likely reflux.,INDICATIONS: The patient is a 64-year-old male with a history of dysphagia for the past several months. He has been on proton pump inhibitor therapy without significant relief. He is referred for EGD.,PROCEDURE IN DETAIL: The patient was brought to the endoscopy suite. After informed consent was obtained, the patient was placed in the left lateral decubitus position. Topical anesthesia was applied to the oropharynx. The endoscope was passed without difficulty. The esophagus was examined and found to have severe linear erosions in the distal esophagus consistent with reflux esophagitis. The stomach was examined and was normal. The duodenum was examined to the second portion and was normal. Biopsies were taken from the distal esophagus. The patient tolerated the procedure well.

Example 2: Neurology
CHIEF COMPLAINT:, Headache.,HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:, The patient is a 34-year-old female who presents with a chief complaint of headache for the last three months. She describes the headache as a constant, dull ache located in the bilateral frontal regions. She states the pain is worse in the morning and improves throughout the day. She denies any photophobia or phonophobia. She has tried over-the-counter analgesics without relief. She denies any history of trauma. She denies any focal weakness or numbness. She denies any change in vision.,PAST MEDICAL HISTORY:, Migraines.,FAMILY HISTORY:, Mother with history of migraines.,NEUROLOGICAL EXAMINATION:, The patient is alert and oriented. Cranial nerves II-XII are intact. Motor strength is 5/5 in all extremities. Sensation is intact to light touch. Cerebellar exam is normal. Gait is normal.

Example 3: Urology
HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS: , The patient is a 63-year-old male with a history of benign prostatic hyperplasia, who presents for followup. He has been on Flomax for the past year with good results. He reports that his urinary stream has improved and he is not getting up at night to urinate. He denies any hematuria, dysuria, or urgency. He is here for a routine followup and prostate check.,PAST MEDICAL HISTORY: , Benign prostatic hyperplasia, hypertension.,MEDICATIONS: , Flomax, lisinopril.,DIGITAL RECTAL EXAM: , The prostate is smooth, firm, and nontender. There are no nodules palpated. The size is estimated at 30 grams.,ASSESSMENT AND PLAN: , Benign prostatic hyperplasia, stable on Flomax. We will continue the current medication. The patient will return in one year for followup.
